Objective
In this capstone project, you will apply time series forecasting and
analysis techniques to select stocks and allocate capital within a
virtual ₹10,00,000 portfolio on the StockGro platform. You will build
predictive models (ARIMA, Prophet, LSTM, etc.) on historical NSE
stock data, use those models to make informed investment
decisions, execute those decisions on the StockGro virtual trading
platform, and finally compare your model's predictions against the
actual market outcomes observed on StockGro. The entire workflow
— from data analysis to live virtual trading to final reporting —
constitutes your submission.
Platform Background
StockGro is a virtual stock market simulator that uses live NSE/BSE
data. It allows users to build portfolios, allocate funds, execute
trades, and monitor real-time performance — all without any real
financial risk. You will use StockGro as the execution environment
to test whether your data-driven forecasts translate into actual
portfolio gains.

Step-by-Step Project Tasks
Follow the tasks below in order. Each task builds on the previous one.
Task 1: Stock Universe Selection
What you must do:
Choose stocks from diverse sectors (e.g., Banking, IT, Pharma,
FMCG, Auto) to ensure portfolio diversification.
Justify each stock selection using at least one of the following
data-driven approaches:
Rolling standard deviation to identify stocks with interesting
volatility profiles
Seasonal decomposition to detect stocks with strong trends
Sector-based rationale (e.g., selecting from a high-
momentum sector)
Deliverable: A written justification for your chosen stock universe.
Task 2: Data Preprocessing
Before modeling, prepare your data so it is clean and suitable for
time series analysis.
What you must do:
Handle missing values using forward fill, backward fill, or
removal
Test each stock's price series for stationarity using the
Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) test; apply differencing if the
series is non-stationary
Normalize or scale data if you plan to use ML-based models like
LSTM
Split the data into training (before July 2025) and testing (July
2025 – December 2025) sets
Deliverable: Clean, preprocessed time series datasets for each
selected stock, ready for modeling.
For each model:
Train on the training set
Validate on the test set (last 6 months)
Forecast prices for the next 5 trading days
Evaluate using MAPE, RMSE, and/or directional accuracy
(did the model correctly predict whether the price goes up
or down?)
Tune ARIMA using AIC/BIC criteria and validate residuals
Optionally, combine multiple model predictions into an
ensemble for better accuracy
Deliverable: Forecasted prices for each stock over the next 2 trading
days, along with model evaluation metrics.
Task 3: Time Series Forecasting
Build forecasting models to predict stock prices for the next 2
trading days beyond your dataset (i.e., into early January 2026, or
equivalently, the 2 days of the StockGro trading window).
What you must do:
Implement at least one (preferably two or more) of the following
models:
ARIMA / SARIMA
Exponential Smoothing (SES / Holt-Winters)
Facebook Prophet
LSTM / GRU
CNN-based time series model
Any transformer-based or pretrained model
Deliverable: Volatility estimates and trend assessments for each
stock in your universe, which will directly feed into your portfolio
allocation strategy in the next task.
Task 4:  Volatility and Trend Analysis
Beyond just price forecasts, you must understand each stock's risk
profile and trend behavior.
What you must do:
Volatility Estimation:
Compute log returns for each stock
Analyze fluctuations in log returns to estimate short-term risk
Optionally use GARCH(1,1) or rolling standard deviation to
model time-varying volatility and forecast near-term
uncertainty
Trend and Pattern Analysis:
Apply seasonal decomposition (e.g., STL decomposition) to
break each stock's price series into trend, seasonality, and
residual/noise components
Evaluate the strength and direction of the trend to understand
whether a stock is in an upward, downward, or sideways phase
Strategy D — Sector Momentum Rotation:
Group your selected stocks by sector
Assess which sectors are showing consistent upward momentum
Allocate more capital to high-momentum sectors, and within each sector,
use one of the above strategies to finalize individual stock weights
Deliverable: A final portfolio allocation table showing: stock name, allocated
amount (in ₹), weight (%), and the reasoning/strategy behind the allocation.
Task 5: Portfolio Construction and Capital Allocation
Using the outputs of Tasks 3 and 4, decide how to allocate your ₹10,00,000
virtual capital across your selected stocks. You must use at least two of the
following strategies:
Strategy A — Forecast-Guided Allocation:
Rank stocks by their predicted 5-day return from your forecasting models
Allocate more capital to stocks with higher predicted gains and higher
forecast confidence
Optionally adjust weights based on model uncertainty or prediction
intervals
Strategy B — Volatility-Aware Sizing:
Use predicted volatility (from GARCH or rolling std dev) to assign weights
inversely proportional to volatility: wᵢ = (1/σ̂ ᵢ) / Σⱼ(1/σ̂ ⱼ)
Stocks expected to be more volatile receive a smaller allocation; more stable
stocks receive a larger share
Normalize all weights to sum to 1
Strategy C — Correlation-Based Diversification:
Compute a correlation matrix across your selected stocks using recent
return windows
Prefer combinations of stocks that are lowly correlated with each other to
reduce portfolio-level risk
Avoid concentrating capital in highly correlated stocks
Task 6: Model Comparison
Compare all the forecasting models you built in Task 3.
What you must do:
Tabulate MAPE, RMSE, and directional accuracy for each model
across all stocks
Comment on each model's strengths and weaknesses (e.g.,
ARIMA is interpretable but struggles with non-linearity; LSTM
captures complex patterns but requires more data and tuning)
Discuss whether an ensemble approach improved results
State clearly which model(s) you ultimately relied on for your
portfolio decisions and why
Deliverable: A comparative model evaluation table and a written
discussion.
